Don't go chasing waterfalls - chase rainbows instead.

Heading into another 4 weeks of lockdown, after living through 4 weeks of lockdown, I allowed myself to go to a safe social distancing, 10km park and waterfront to do some mental running aka: reading. I took my trustry the design of everyday things soaked in fresh air and sun whilst reading up on mental and conceptual models. When I looked up there was a rainbow in the far distance, whilst the warm sun rays hitting my skin. This polarity made me think about the polarity of mental models, and what I can now do to further understand this concept during user research to navigate this; including my own mental model whilst conducting user research - so meta!.

Really really wanna zigazig ha -

I practised the 80/20 rule today - you know the Pareto Principle? an aphorism which says: 80% of outcomes result from 20% of all causes for any given event. This concept has been swelling around in my head lately, I am huge fan of working smartly, not harder. Because of my Leo Sun energy, I love to play, even when I'm working - I like to have fun doing so. Many have judged me for these, but more have been inspired by this. The 80/20 principle allows us to work smart and win bigger - uhm yes please. Thing is, this can be applied for anything - I love seeing it in Design aka the white space - I'm practising this everyday as a social experiment through August - let's see how this cooks up.
